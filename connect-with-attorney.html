Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about how the network works.

Does The Comp Desk recommend specific attorneys?
No. The Comp Desk does not endorse, recommend, or certify any attorney in the network. Attorney assignment is mechanical and based on neutral round-robin rotation. You are responsible for evaluating attorneys and choosing one that fits your needs.
How are attorneys assigned to my case?
Attorneys are assigned through neutral round-robin rotation based on your county. There is no bidding, no rankings, and no paid priority. When you submit your case information, the next participating attorney in your county's rotation receives a notification. If they don't respond within 48 hours, the lead automatically moves to the next attorney.
Why is the owner's firm (Shulman & Hill) excluded?
To avoid conflict of interest. Joel Mays, the creator of The Comp Desk, is an attorney at Shulman & Hill. To ensure this service remains neutral and unbiased, the firm is permanently excluded — enforced by automated testing in the code. The Comp Desk is a connection tool, not a marketing vehicle for the owner's law practice.
How much does it cost for attorneys to participate?
Attorneys purchase lead credits. A single credit costs $99. Volume packages are available: 3 credits for $249 (Starter), 5 credits for $449 (Standard), or 10 credits for $799 (Volume). One credit is used per accepted lead. There are no monthly fees, no setup fees, and no referral percentages. Injured workers use the service completely free of charge.
What if I accept a lead but can't reach the worker?
If you document 3 contact attempts over 5 business days and the worker remains unreachable, you receive a full credit refund. Contact joel@thecompdesk.com with your attempt log to request the refund.
Can I request removal from the network?
Yes. Any attorney can request removal from the network by contacting The Comp Desk at joel@thecompdesk.com. Removal is processed within one business day. Unused credits are refundable. No questions asked.
