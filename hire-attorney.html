Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about how the directory works.

Does The Comp Desk recommend specific attorneys?
No. The Comp Desk does not endorse, recommend, or certify any attorney listed in the directory. Listing is purely informational. You are responsible for evaluating attorneys and choosing one that fits your needs. Check their experience, reviews, and whether they're a good fit for you.
How are attorneys ordered in the directory?
Every visitor sees attorneys in a random order, reshuffled on every page load using Fisher-Yates shuffle. There is no "first slot" to buy, no priority placement, and no paid positioning. Random rotation ensures equal visibility for every attorney regardless of how long they've been listed or how much they pay.
Why is the owner's firm (Shulman & Hill) excluded from the directory?
To avoid conflict of interest. Joel Mays, the creator of The Comp Desk, is a partner at Shulman & Hill. To ensure this directory remains neutral and unbiased, the firm is permanently excluded — enforced by automated testing in the code. The Comp Desk is a referral tool, not a marketing vehicle for the owner's law practice.
How much does it cost for attorneys to be listed?
Attorney listings cost $5.99/month. This flat fee covers directory maintenance, platform hosting, and infrastructure costs. There are no setup fees, referral fees, or variable pricing based on performance. Injured workers use the directory completely free of charge. No one pays per click or lead.
Can I request removal or modification of my listing?
Yes. Any attorney can request removal, suspension, or modification of their listing by contacting The Comp Desk at joel@thecompdesk.com. Requests are processed within one business day. No questions asked. You can pause your listing anytime or remove it permanently.
