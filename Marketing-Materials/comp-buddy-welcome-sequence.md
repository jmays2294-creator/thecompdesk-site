## Email 2 — Send: +2 days
**Subject:** The 3 things that trip people up first
**Preheader:** Comp Buddy's "don't do this" shortlist.

Hey, it's Comp Buddy.

Quick one today. After watching a *lot* of comp claims, three mistakes show
up over and over in the early days:

1. **Waiting to report the injury.** The clock starts ticking the moment it
   happens. Reporting late gives the insurance carrier a reason to question
   the whole claim.
2. **Telling the doctor "I'm fine" when you're not.** Doctors write down what
   you say. Then the carrier reads it. Be honest about what hurts.
3. **Signing things you haven't read.** If a form shows up in the mail and
   you don't know what it is — pause. Ask. (You can always ask me.)

That's it for today. Short and sweet.

Comp Buddy 🐾

## Email 3 — Send: +4 days
**Subject:** What "AWW" actually means (and why you should care)
**Preheader:** Comp Buddy decodes the most important number on your claim.

Hi friend —

Today's word: **AWW**. Stands for *Average Weekly Wage*. It's the single
number the whole claim is built on, because almost every check you might
receive is calculated as a percentage of it.

Here's the catch: insurance carriers don't always calculate it the way they
should. Overtime, second jobs, bonuses — those can all count, and a lot of
people leave money on the table because nobody told them.

If you want to ballpark yours, Comp Buddy's calculators can help. If the
number the carrier gave you feels low, that's worth a second look.

I've got two more notes coming. Stay tuned.

Comp Buddy 🐾

## Email 4 — Send: +7 days
**Subject:** When it's time to bring in a human
**Preheader:** Comp Buddy is great. Comp Buddy is also a mascot.

Hey —

Real talk for a second. I'm a mascot. A friendly, well-informed mascot, but a
mascot. There are moments in a workers' comp claim where you genuinely need a
human attorney in your corner — somebody who can show up at hearings, file
motions, and push back on the carrier directly.

Some signs it might be that moment:

- You got a notice scheduling a hearing.
- The carrier denied or controverted your claim.
- Your benefits stopped or got cut.
- An IME (independent medical exam) is on the calendar.
- You're being asked to settle and you don't know if the number is fair.

If any of those are happening, the find-an-attorney directory on The Comp
Desk is a good next step. No pressure — just want you to know the door is
there.

Comp Buddy 🐾
