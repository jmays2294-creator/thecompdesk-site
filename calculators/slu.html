About Schedule Loss of Use (SLU)

Schedule Loss of Use (SLU) is a fixed benefit available to injured workers in New York State under Workers' Compensation Law §15(3). Unlike permanent partial disability benefits, SLU provides a predetermined number of weeks of benefits based on the specific body part injured and the degree of permanent loss of use.

How SLU Works: The law assigns a maximum number of weeks to each body part. For example, an arm injury has a maximum of 312 weeks, while an eye injury has 160 weeks. The benefit is calculated by multiplying the percentage of loss of use by the maximum weeks for that body part, then multiplying by the worker's compensation rate.

SLU awards are typically made in cases where the worker has reached maximum medical improvement and the degree of permanent loss of use can be established. The Workers' Compensation Board determines the percentage based on medical evidence, functional capacity evaluations, and statutory guidelines.
