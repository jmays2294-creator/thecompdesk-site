About Schedule Loss of Use (SLU)

Schedule Loss of Use (SLU) is a fixed benefit available to injured workers in New York State under Workers' Compensation Law §15(3). Unlike permanent partial disability benefits, which vary by case, SLU provides a predetermined number of weeks of benefits based on the specific body part injured and the degree of permanent loss of use.

How SLU Works: The workers' compensation law assigns a maximum number of weeks to each body part. For example, an arm injury has a maximum of 312 weeks, while an eye injury has 160 weeks. The actual benefit is calculated by multiplying the percentage of loss of use (0-100%) by the maximum weeks for that body part, then multiplying by the worker's compensation rate (typically 2/3 of their average weekly wage, subject to the statutory maximum).

Schedule Loss of Use Benefits: SLU benefits are available for permanent injuries to specific body parts including arms, hands, legs, feet, fingers, toes, thumbs, eyes, and ears. The schedule of benefits is contained in WCL §15(3) and is adjusted annually by the Department of Financial Services based on cost-of-living changes.

SLU awards are typically made in cases where the worker has reached maximum medical improvement and the degree of permanent loss of use can be established. The Workers' Compensation Board determines the percentage of loss of use based on medical evidence, functional capacity evaluations, and statutory guidelines.
