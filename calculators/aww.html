About Average Weekly Wage (AWW)

Average Weekly Wage (AWW) is the foundation of New York State workers' compensation benefits. Established under Workers' Compensation Law §14, AWW determines the compensation rate that an injured worker receives during periods of disability. The compensation rate is calculated as two-thirds (2/3) of the AWW, subject to the statutory maximum rate set by the Department of Financial Services.

How AWW is Calculated: For most workers, AWW is computed by taking the worker's gross earnings during the 52 weeks preceding the injury and dividing by 52. This includes all earnings from the employer at the time of injury, including regular wages, overtime, tips, bonuses, and other forms of compensation. For workers who have not worked a full 52 weeks, the law provides methods to calculate AWW based on available earnings history.

Compensation Rate Calculation: Once the AWW is determined, the workers' compensation rate is the lessor of: (1) two-thirds of the AWW, or (2) the maximum weekly benefit rate set annually by the New York Department of Financial Services. The maximum rate is adjusted each July 1st based on wage trends and cost-of-living changes.

Accurate calculation of AWW is critical because it directly impacts all benefits the worker receives, including temporary disability benefits, permanent disability benefits, and vocational rehabilitation benefits. Disputes over AWW calculations are common in workers' compensation cases and often require detailed wage history analysis and legal arguments before the Workers' Compensation Board.
