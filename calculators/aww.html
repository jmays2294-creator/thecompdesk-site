About Average Weekly Wage (AWW)

Average Weekly Wage (AWW) is the foundation of New York State workers' compensation benefits. Established under Workers' Compensation Law §14, AWW determines the compensation rate an injured worker receives during periods of disability. The compensation rate is calculated as two-thirds (2/3) of the AWW, subject to the statutory maximum rate set annually by the Department of Financial Services.

How AWW is Calculated: For most workers, AWW is computed by taking gross earnings during the 52 weeks preceding the injury and dividing by 52. This includes regular wages, overtime, tips, bonuses, and other compensation. For workers who have not worked a full 52 weeks, the law provides methods to calculate AWW based on available earnings history.

Concurrent Employment: Under WCL §14, wages from all concurrent employment at the time of injury are combined to calculate AWW. If a worker held two jobs when injured, both sets of wages are included in the AWW calculation — even if only one employer's work caused the injury.

Accurate calculation of AWW is critical because it directly impacts all benefits the worker receives, including temporary disability benefits, permanent disability benefits, and vocational rehabilitation benefits. Disputes over AWW calculations are common and often require detailed wage history analysis and legal argument before the Workers' Compensation Board.
