About Classified Awards in New York

Classified awards are formal determinations by the Workers' Compensation Board that establish ongoing benefits for permanently disabled workers. The three main types of classifications in New York are Permanent Partial Disability (PPD), Permanent Total Disability (PTD), and Death Benefits.

Permanent Partial Disability (PPD): PPD classifications are issued when a worker has reached maximum medical improvement and has sustained a permanent injury that reduces wage earning capacity but can still work in some capacity. The PPD award consists of a weekly benefit based on a percentage of loss of wage earning capacity, multiplied by the compensation rate.

Permanent Total Disability (PTD): A PTD classification is issued when the worker is permanently and completely unable to work due to the work injury. PTD benefits continue for life or until the worker returns to work, providing a full weekly benefit equal to the compensation rate.

Death Benefits: When a worker dies as a result of a work-related injury, eligible dependents may receive weekly benefits and funeral expense reimbursement. Calculations depend on the number of eligible dependents.
