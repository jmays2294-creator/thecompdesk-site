        About CCP Awards in New York
      

      

        

          A Classified Compensation Period (CCP) award is a formal determination by the Workers' Compensation Board that establishes a period of indemnity benefits. Awards are built period-by-period, each with its own benefit type (designation) and dates.
        

        

          TT — Temporary Total: Rate = 2/3 of AWW, capped at the statutory maximum for the date of accident. Used when the claimant is totally disabled from all work.
        

        

          RE — Reduced Earnings: Rate = 2/3 × (AWW − current weekly earnings). Used when the claimant returns to lighter duty at reduced wages.
        

        

          TR — Temporary Reduced: Rate = a percentage of the TT rate, entered manually. Used by stipulation or direction.
        

        

          TP / NCLT / NME: Manual rate entry. TP = temporary partial; NCLT = no-comp lost time; NME = no medical evidence.
        

        

          Attorney Fee (WCL §24): The fee on the award equals 15% of the moving (new money) amount. The fee on the CCP is 1/3 of the CCP dollar amount. Both are paid from claimant's award.
