Understanding NYS Maximum Compensation Rates

The maximum weekly compensation rate in New York State is the highest amount a workers' compensation insurance carrier can pay to an injured worker for temporary or permanent disability benefits. This rate is adjusted annually by the New York Department of Financial Services, effective July 1st each year. The adjustment is based on wage indices calculated from data provided by the U.S. Department of Labor.

How Rates Apply: The maximum rate that applies to a specific workers' compensation case is determined by the date of the work-related injury or occupational disease. Once a rate is established for a case based on the date of injury, that rate remains applicable throughout the life of the case. If a worker receives benefits spanning multiple rate years, the applicable rate depends on when the injury occurred, not when benefits are being paid.

Impact on Benefits: The maximum rate affects all types of workers' compensation benefits. For example, a worker's 2/3 compensation rate (calculated as two-thirds of Average Weekly Wage) cannot exceed the statutory maximum for the year of injury. This means that workers with higher wages are capped at the maximum rate, while lower-wage workers receive 2/3 of their AWW regardless of the maximum.

It is important to use the correct rate for the date of injury when calculating or estimating workers' compensation benefits. Using an incorrect rate can lead to significant overestimates or underestimates of benefit amounts. When in doubt, consult with a workers' compensation professional or attorney to ensure the correct rate is being applied to your case.
