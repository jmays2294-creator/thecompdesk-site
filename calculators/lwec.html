About Loss of Wage Earning Capacity (LWEC)

Loss of Wage Earning Capacity (LWEC), also known as permanent partial disability (PPD) in some contexts, is a form of workers' compensation benefit available to injured workers in New York State who have suffered permanent injuries that reduce their ability to earn wages. Unlike Schedule Loss of Use, which is a fixed benefit, LWEC is flexible and based on the individual worker's actual loss of earning capacity.

Classification System: When an injured worker reaches maximum medical improvement and cannot return to regular employment, the Workers' Compensation Board may classify the worker as having lost a percentage of their wage earning capacity. This classification is expressed as a percentage (e.g., 25%, 50%, 75% loss) based on the worker's medical condition, age, education, skills, and job market conditions.

LWEC Benefit Calculation: The weekly LWEC benefit is calculated by multiplying the loss of earning capacity percentage by the worker's compensation rate. For example, if a worker has a 50% loss of earning capacity and a compensation rate of $750/week, the LWEC benefit would be $375/week. LWEC benefits continue until the worker reaches age 65 or returns to work, depending on the terms of the classification.
